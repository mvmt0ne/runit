07 — Voice & Tone

Direct. Precise. Forged
for the mission.

DO
"Powering actionable insights for America's defense and transportation systems."
Active verbs, specific audience, mission framing. No fluff.
DON'T
"We provide innovative solutions that help organizations leverage data-driven insights across various verticals."
Avoid buzzwords, passive voice, vague industry jargon, and anything that sounds like a generic tech company.
DO
"Analyze serial bus traffic and RF data to flag threats in near real-time."
Technical specificity builds credibility. Name the technology. State the outcome.
DON'T
"Our cutting-edge AI-powered platform enables comprehensive monitoring for enhanced security posture."
Avoid superlatives, marketing language, and anything that obscures rather than clarifies capability.
08 — Design Principles

Rules for staying
on-brand.

✓ Do
  • Use #FF5841 orange sparingly — for primary CTAs and hero backgrounds only
  • Keep border-radius at 0 for all interactive elements
  • Use monospace font for all data, metrics, and technical readouts
  • Apply split-screen layouts with strong contrast between panels
  • Use all-caps + letter-spacing for labels and navigation items
  • Include zero-padded numbered lists (01. 02. 03.) for sequential content
✗ Don't
  • Use rounded corners, drop shadows, or gradients — they undercut the industrial tone
  • Mix more than 2 typefaces — NonSans + AuxMono is the system
  • Use blue, green, or purple as accent colors — orange is the single accent
  • Over-animate UI elements — motion should feel functional, not decorative
  • Use lowercase for navigation or button labels
  • Use icons or illustrations — photography (desaturated) or pure typography only
